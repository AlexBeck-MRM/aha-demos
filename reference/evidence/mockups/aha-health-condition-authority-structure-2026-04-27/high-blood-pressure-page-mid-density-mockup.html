911

Very high reading with symptoms?

If your blood pressure is higher than 180/120 mm Hg, check it again after one minute. Call 911 if it is still that high and you have chest pain, shortness of breath, weakness, vision changes, difficulty speaking, or another serious symptom.

Overview

High blood pressure at a glance

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against the walls of your arteries. Repeated high readings need attention, even if you feel well.

Key things to know

  • High blood pressure often has no symptoms.
  • Your numbers matter even when you feel well.
  • Small daily changes can help.
  • Medication may be part of the plan.

Why it matters

Uncontrolled high blood pressure can quietly damage blood vessels and raise the risk of stroke, heart attack, heart failure, kidney disease, and vision loss.

Symptoms

High blood pressure is often silent

Symptoms are not a reliable way to know whether your blood pressure is high. The only way to know is to measure it.

Get urgent help if a very high reading comes with

  • chest pain or shortness of breath
  • numbness, weakness, or vision changes
  • difficulty speaking, confusion, or fainting

Track before you need it

Keep recent readings, time of day, medications, missed doses, symptoms, and questions for your care team.

Causes

What can raise blood pressure

High blood pressure usually does not have one simple cause. It can be shaped by age, family history, health conditions, medicines, sleep, stress, access to care, and everyday habits.

Common contributors

  • family history, age, diabetes, kidney disease, or sleep apnea
  • eating patterns high in sodium or not enough activity
  • alcohol, tobacco, stress, poor sleep, or some medicines

Home monitoring basics

  • Use a validated upper-arm monitor.
  • Sit with feet flat and arm supported.
  • Avoid caffeine, smoking, and exercise beforehand.

Treatment

Care options that can help

Treatment usually combines regular monitoring, daily habits, and sometimes medication. Some people begin with lifestyle changes. Some need medication right away. Many people need both.

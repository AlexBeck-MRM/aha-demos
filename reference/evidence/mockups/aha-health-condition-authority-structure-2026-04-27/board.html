Subpage navigation

Specific pages are visible, but not part of the main chapter list

The sidebar should not become a second sitemap. It should show where you are, how to go back, and the few deeper pages that matter in the current chapter.

The problem with putting every subpage in the sidebar

If specialist pages sit directly under the six chapters, the fixed health-reference structure starts to collapse back into the old tree. The user has to parse hierarchy instead of reading.

Avoid this pattern

  • Overview
    • Top 10
    • Myths
    • Low blood pressure
  • Treatment
    • Medications
    • Resistant hypertension
    • Pulmonary hypertension
  • Living with it
    • Diet
    • Salt
    • Stress
    • Weight
Rule: chapter nav is stable. Subpage links are contextual, separated, and limited.

Condition guide structure

The guide has to answer the user’s next question, not just describe the condition.

Patients, caregivers, and health-conscious consumers arrive with different levels of fear, knowledge, and urgency. The page needs one stable path that can still support all of them.

The structure scales because it separates page navigation from deeper content.

The main guide stays consistent. Condition-specific detail appears as modules, accordions, tools, related content, or subpages inside the relevant section.

“Do I have this?”

Diagnosis holds tests, numbers, stages, and confirmation.

“Is this urgent?”

Symptoms holds red flags and immediate actions.

“Why did this happen?”

Causes holds contributors, risks, and prevention context.

“How do I treat it?”

Treatment holds clinical options and care-team guidance.

“How do I help someone?”

Caregiver prompts live inside Symptoms, Treatment, and Daily Life.

“What should I change?”

Daily Life holds habits, routines, tools, and support.

Reusable page logic

Sidebar

Shows only the six fixed sections so the page remains easy to scan and navigate.

Section body

Holds summaries, accordions, tools, healthy-living links, and condition-specific guidance.

Deep pages

Stay contextual. They link back to the parent guide and the section they belong to.

Other audiences

Professionals, donors, and volunteers get clear exits and proof cues without reshaping the patient guide.

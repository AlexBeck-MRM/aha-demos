# Forms

## Direction
Forms should feel calm, spacious, and trustworthy rather than transactional and brittle. Use clear labels, visible field boundaries, forgiving spacing, and strong focus and error states so sign-up, donation, and support flows feel low-friction.

## Reasoning
- AHA asks for meaningful actions, and form confidence has a direct effect on completion.
- Tactile fields reduce intimidation and make the system feel better cared for.
- Clear guidance and recovery states matter more than decorative styling in high-intent flows.
- This section still needs real board examples before the form language can be fully resolved.

## Guardrail
Do not let visual softness reduce accessibility contrast or field clarity.

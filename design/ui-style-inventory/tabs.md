# Tabs

## Direction
Tabs should feel large enough to trust, clearly segmented, and visibly selected. Use depth, colour, and spacing to make the active tab unmistakable while keeping inactive tabs available rather than disabled-looking.

## Reasoning
- Strong state contrast improves orientation and reduces ambiguity in dense content groups.
- Tabs are one of the clearest places to express tactility in the system.
- Distinct primary and secondary tab behaviors can help hierarchy without changing the underlying language.
- Faint or flat inactive states make the pattern feel less dependable than it needs to be.

## Route emphasis
Route A can feel slightly softer. Route B can feel slightly more crisp and system-led.

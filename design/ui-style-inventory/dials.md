# Dials

## Direction
Use dial-like sliders or progress scrubs only when a continuous range or sequence genuinely helps people understand status, browsing, or progress. Keep them simple, tactile, and secondary to the content they are guiding.

## Reasoning
- A scrubber can turn a long sequence into a more legible interaction when the user needs to browse through steps or tips.
- The control should clarify the sequence, not become the point of attention.
- Tactile handling can make these modules feel useful and deliberate rather than generic.
- We still need to decide where AHA truly needs this pattern versus a simpler list or carousel.

## Guardrail
Do not use ornamental dials where a simple number or chart communicates better.

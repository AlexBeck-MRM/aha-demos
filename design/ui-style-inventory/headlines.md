# Headlines

## Direction
Headlines should be direct, specific, and human rather than slogan-heavy. Keep them short, readable, and confident, with enough warmth to feel like guidance instead of institutional broadcast.

## Reasoning
- Clear headlines reduce scanning effort and help people understand the point of a section faster.
- Specific language supports a broader range of reading confidence than abstract or promotional phrasing.
- Human tone matters because AHA is trying to feel like a capable guide, not a distant repository.
- Overblown display language quickly makes dense pages harder to trust.

# Video

## Direction
Use video for human stories, practical demonstrations, or moments where motion adds understanding that a still image cannot. Keep playback controlled, editing gentle, and thumbnails strong enough to do the initial persuasion.

## Reasoning
- Video can add humanity and explanation, but it also carries attention and performance cost.
- Calm playback behavior matters more than visual spectacle in this ecosystem.
- Strong thumbnails and preview states let people choose rather than be dragged into playback.
- We still need to decide when ambient motion is useful versus when a still image is the clearer choice.

## Guardrail
Always provide a static fallback and avoid using video as the only carrier of meaning.

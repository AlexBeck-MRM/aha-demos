# Color Scheme

## Direction
Keep the interface white-first and shift the illustration palette away from the warmer brown-stone base. The core family should come from the attached references: white, off-white, cool bone, pale grey stone, taupe-grey, charcoal, AHA red, and deep red. Red remains the decisive AHA signal. Light pastels are allowed only as secondary situation colours for nature, water, produce, sleep, or wellbeing details, not as a general accent system.

## Reasoning
- The attached palette reference is strongest when it behaves like a monochrome-stone system with AHA red inside it, not when the taupe and warm stone take over the scene.
- The canonical brief still needs a bright, warm, trustworthy system, but that warmth should come from people, gesture, spacing, and small material details rather than a brown cast across every illustration.
- Pale greys and cool bones keep the illustrations cleaner beside white UI surfaces and reduce the risk of generic beige wellness illustration.
- AHA red should carry emphasis, action, and emotional anchor points; deep red exists only to add depth inside red objects.
- Secondary pastels can help topic comprehension, especially nature and water, but they should stay pale enough that red remains the brand signal.

## Locked illustration palette
- White `#ffffff`: page canvas, board background, and open negative space.
- Off white `#f4f3f3`: lightest illustration support surfaces.
- Soft white `#f5f4f2`: warm-white planes where pure white needs separation.
- Cool bone `#eeece9`: default pale neutral for bodies, cards, and large support fields.
- Light stone grey `#e1e0dc`: secondary planes, soft depth, and quiet object separation.
- Warm stone `#ddd8d2`: limited warmth for skin-adjacent support, food, wood, or moments that would feel too cold without it.
- Taupe grey `#7d7971`: muted props, clothing, hair, and secondary definition where charcoal would feel too strong.
- Charcoal `#3d3c3a`: crisp definition points, hair, device details, and small functional marks.
- Black `#000000`: rare high-contrast marks only; do not use as a broad fill.
- AHA red `#e6001b`: key clothing, signals, emphasis objects, selected UI cues, and emotional anchor moments.
- Deep red `#bf0000`: selective shadow or depth inside red elements only.

## Secondary pastels
- Water / air tint `#edf2f3`: water, sky, breath, cool clinical support, and light environmental fields.
- Nature tint `#e7f0eb`: leaves, plants, walking paths, outdoor context, and calm wellbeing cues.
- Soft red tint `#f6e8e9`: quiet halo around red actions, alerts, or support moments where a full red fill would be too strong.
- Pale oat `#f5ebdd`: grains, food, wood, or seasonal warmth only; use sparingly because it can pull the system back toward beige.

## Colour rationale
- The pale grey-bone range does most of the system work so the illustrations stay human without drifting into peach, clay, or brown ambience.
- Red is the anchor, not the atmosphere.
- Pastels are topic helpers, not brand colours.
- The palette should stay intentionally narrow; casual extra creams, peaches, tans, or saturated topic colours will make the system softer and less ownable than intended.

## Guardrail
- Do not add extra sub-tones unless the motif genuinely needs them.
- Keep shadows broad and tonal. No airbrushed shading, hard drop-shadow logic, or saturated blue-grey healthcare cast.
- Do not use red as a field wash across entire scenes or large interface backgrounds.
- Do not let taupe, warm stone, oat, skin warmth, or wood tones become the dominant read of an illustration.

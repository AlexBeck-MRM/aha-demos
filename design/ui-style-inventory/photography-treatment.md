# Photography Treatment

## Direction
Use photography that feels warm, believable, and lived-in rather than glossy or generic. Favor caregiving, family, cooking, community, recovery, and clinician moments with natural light, clear focal subjects, and respectful crops.

## Reasoning
- Photography is carrying much of the trust and human proof layer in the system.
- Everyday specificity feels more credible than polished stock perfection.
- Warmth should come from light, intimacy, and real situations rather than heavy grading.
- We still need to decide how much visual consistency the image library needs before it starts feeling repetitive.

## Guardrail
Avoid bland smiling-team imagery and over-filtered wellness photography.

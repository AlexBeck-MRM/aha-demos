# Interaction

## Direction
Interactions should feel calm, responsive, and slightly tactile. Press states can dip or darken to confirm contact, while hover should sharpen clarity or readiness without making the interface jump around.

## Reasoning
- Stable feedback makes a dense system feel more attentive and easier to trust.
- Tactile interactions reinforce the broader depth language in a useful way.
- Subtle motion is enough; the goal is confirmation, not entertainment.
- Overactive hover or press behavior would make the system feel nervous rather than confident.

## Guardrail
Avoid ornamental motion that distracts from reading or task completion.

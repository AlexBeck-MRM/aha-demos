# Nav Elements

## Direction
Keep navigation floating and spatial on first arrival, with core wayfinding separated from high-priority utilities. Search should stay visibly open, Donate should read as distinct but not dominant, and the bar can simplify into a calmer sticky state on scroll.

## Reasoning
- Floating navigation makes the site feel lighter and less institutional than a browser-edge bar.
- Splitting navigation from Search and Donate clarifies purpose and reduces competition inside the header.
- An already-open search field lowers friction in a broad ecosystem where people often arrive with a specific question.
- A simpler sticky state on scroll preserves orientation without keeping the landing-page treatment on screen the whole time.

## Route emphasis
Route A can feel slightly softer and more editorial. Route B can feel slightly cleaner and more engineered.

## Open questions
How much motion or compression should occur on scroll before the nav starts to feel fussy?

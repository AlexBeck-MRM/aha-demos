# Trust Cues

## Direction
Build trust through visible proof that the content is cared for: authorship, review status, update dates, evidence links, and steady interaction behavior. These cues should sit inside the page flow, not show up as a separate layer of institutional badges.

## Reasoning
- Health guidance needs fast credibility signals before people commit to reading or acting.
- Review labels, dates, and source pathways help users judge freshness and authority at a glance.
- Embedded proof feels more honest and readable than stacking seals, statistics, and logos around the content.
- Consistent behavior and clean structure reinforce trust as much as explicit proof labels do.

## Guardrail
Avoid overloading pages with institutional logos, statistics, and boilerplate at the expense of readability.

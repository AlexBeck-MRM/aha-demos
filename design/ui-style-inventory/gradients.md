# Gradients

## Direction
Use broad, low-contrast gradients on large colour surfaces so the interface feels lit rather than flat. They are most useful on AHA red and larger utility panels where a solid fill would otherwise feel blunt or too promotional.

## Reasoning
- Gradients add spatial presence without needing heavier chrome or louder effects.
- They soften big colour blocks while keeping the overall system bright and controlled.
- This works best as a structural surface treatment, not as a default fill style.
- Low contrast is key; if the gradient becomes flashy, trust and readability both drop.

## Guardrail
Do not use gradients as a default fill style. Avoid neon, rainbow, or high-contrast hotspot treatments, and do not let gradients reduce text contrast or make utility modules feel atmospheric when they need to feel precise.

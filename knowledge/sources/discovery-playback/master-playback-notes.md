## Source

## What the source establishes
- The project is about relationship-building in an age of disconnection and disruption, not only a visual redesign.
- Core goals include relaunching heart.org, selecting and implementing a mature CMS, and creating a digital foundation for broader engagement.
- Discovery included 100+ stakeholders plus UX, competitor, market, document, and data-analysis activities.

## Future experience ambition
- Heart.org should become `a trusted companion that empowers every person on their journey`.
- The desired shift is from passive repository to active companion, from siloed views to seeing someone as a whole person, and from purely informational to empowering and transactional.

## Experience principles surfaced in the deck
- Build trust.
- Operate intelligently.
- Be human.
- Less is more.
- Create frictionless experiences.
- Be the number-one guide for heart health.

## Opportunity areas from the deck
- Define a compelling and unified narrative.
- Foster holistic audience understanding.
- Craft unified and interesting visual brand expression.
- Become a trusted guide.
- Connect experiences into one ecosystem.
- Streamline content for clarity and impact.
- Enable better-timed engagement opportunities.
- Deliver intuitive search.
- Build user-first navigation.

## Visual and experience signals
- Stakeholders see the current site as static, dated, and visually one-dimensional.
- AHA's brand equity is strong, but the site currently feels repetitive and copy-paste.
- The future direction should use dynamic layouts, stronger visual storytelling, more flexible templates, and clearer hierarchy for dense content.

## Definition-phase implications
- Website structure and navigation must be decided deliberately.
- Visual concepting should make the brand work harder in digital.
- Content audit and governance are part of the experience problem, not separate from it.

# AHA Space Brand Appeal Matrix Global Set

## Scope
This note supersedes the smaller US-only matrix pass.

It expands the set to:
- 10 US brands
- 10 international heart-charity brands

## Why expand the set
- The US-only view was useful for seeing the first pattern.
- It did not show enough variation in heart-charity posture outside the US.
- A larger global set gives a better read on how `"guide utility"` and `"human invitation"` combine in category-adjacent brands.

## Forbes review
- Reviewed `Forbes Top Charities 2025` on April 17, 2026 as a scale check for the US half of the set.
- Used it to pressure-test which large US brands clearly operate in the same trust, guidance, donation, or public-service attention space as AHA.
- Did not use it as the only selector because the Forbes list is US-only and the brief here required 10 additional international comparators.
- In practice this kept the US side anchored around large national brands like Red Cross, St. Jude, American Cancer Society, Mayo Clinic, American Diabetes Association, and American Lung Association, then the global side was added separately through direct category review.

## Working axes
- `Guide utility`
- `Human invitation`

## Axis framing
- `Guide utility` asks how strongly the homepage behaves like a practical public guide.
- `Human invitation` asks how much the homepage feels made for people, not just institutions.

The left side of the matrix is not `"cold"`.
It is more:
- institutional
- clinical
- system-like

The right side is more:
- human
- inviting
- companion-led

## Brand goal relevance
This framing is closer to the AHA ambition than `"warm vs cold"` because AHA needs both:
- number-one guide utility
- a more inviting human tone

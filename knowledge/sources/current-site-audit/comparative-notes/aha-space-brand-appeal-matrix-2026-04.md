# AHA Space Brand Appeal Matrix

## Scope
This note defines a working 2D matrix for the current AHA-space competitor set.

The matrix is directional.
It is not a quantitative brand study.

The placement is based on first-fold homepage signals from the captured screenshots:
- logo posture
- hero imagery
- headline tone
- action framing
- navigation and utility density

## Axes
- `Human warmth`
- `Clinical utility`

## Why this pair
- `Human warmth` helps separate brands that feel companion-led, donor-led, or emotionally open.
- `Clinical utility` helps separate brands that feel medically authoritative, task-oriented, or system-like.
- Together they give AHA a more useful read than a single `"clinical vs warm"` line because some brands are high on both and some are low on both.

## Filter groups
- `warm_leaders`
- `balanced_middle`
- `clinical_leaders`

## Working placements
- American Cancer Society: warm leaning, mid utility
- American Red Cross: balanced middle
- St. Jude: strongest warmth signal, lower utility
- Mayo Clinic: strongest clinical utility signal
- Cleveland Clinic: high clinical utility, moderate warmth
- Alzheimer's Association: balanced middle
- Breakthrough T1D: balanced middle with a more contemporary optimistic tone
- American Diabetes Association: utility leaning
- American Lung Association: utility leaning
- Susan G. Komen: warm leaning, lower utility

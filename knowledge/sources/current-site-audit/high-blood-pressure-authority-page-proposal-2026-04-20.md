## Proposal in one line
- move from a `41` page first-hop network to a `1 + 6` model: one canonical authority page for the main job, plus six optional deep-dive exits for truly separate or specialist topics

## What goes on the authority page
- `What is High Blood Pressure?` core explainer
- readings and urgency
- risk factors and symptoms
- body-system impact
- daily management
- medications and care team
- home monitoring and tracking tools
- myths and common questions
- special-population guidance for Black adults and children/teens

## What stays as separate deep dives
- `Low Blood Pressure`
- `Pulmonary Hypertension`
- `Resistant Hypertension`
- full `Heart Attack` explainer
- full `Stroke` explainer
- full `Atherosclerosis` explainer

## Why these stay separate
- they are either adjacent conditions, advanced clinical edge cases, or broader disease-state explainers that would make the main page feel less focused
- they should still be reachable from the authority page, but as secondary exits rather than required next clicks

## Proposed section stack
1. Start here
2. Understand your numbers
3. Risk and symptoms
4. How high blood pressure affects the body
5. Lower it and manage it
6. Chapter guide
7. Special situations
8. Tools and tracking
9. Myths and FAQ
10. Sources and review

## V2 refinement
- page title becomes `Your Guide to High Blood Pressure`
- no hero photography
- use one editorial illustration header built from the live board style
- expose authority at the top:
  - medically reviewed
  - last reviewed date
  - evidence source count
  - emergency threshold
- increase visible text density so the page reads like a real guide, not a sparse landing page
- keep the sticky nav stable, but vary the section compositions so the page rhythm does not feel templated or repetitive
- use expandable chapter blocks so more content can live on the page without turning the first read into a wall of copy

## V2 mockup read
- the seated at-home reading illustration works better than photography because it makes the page feel instructional and ownable at the same time
- moving the review strip to the top materially improves authority and trust
- the larger management chapter now reads like a destination for repeat visits rather than a teaser for more page jumps
- the page rhythm is more varied, but the sticky nav and chapter labels still keep the structure understandable

## Design logic
- use a sticky left section nav so the user can move through the page like a handbook, not like a long undifferentiated article
- start with an intent picker so users can self-sort into the main jobs fast:
  - `Understand my reading`
  - `Lower it`
  - `Is it urgent?`
- pair the title with a top authority strip so the page states its clinical credibility immediately
- use the illustration as an instructional editorial header, not as a decorative mood image
- make the readings section visually stronger than today because number interpretation is one of the clearest user intents
- collapse the `Health Threats` leaf pages into one body-impact section with organ cards and short consequence summaries
- collapse the `How to Manage` leaf pages into one section with tabs, checklists, and a starter plan instead of a dozen route-outs
- add a `Chapter guide` section that acts as the jumping board into expandable subtopics:
  - each chapter starts with a short summary
  - each chapter can expand inline for deeper reading
  - only the adjacent-condition pages stay as full exits
- use a predictable skeleton and an unpredictable cadence:
  - sticky nav and chapter labels stay stable
  - section geometry changes as the content changes
  - some sections are split
  - some are wide and narrative
  - some are accordion-led
- keep authority visible through:
  - clinician review
  - last reviewed date
  - source citations
  - calm medical illustration
  - visible emergency guidance
  - explicit next actions

## Why this should feel more authoritative
- the page becomes the canonical answer, not a signpost farm
- the structure tells a coherent story from definition to action
- the page shows command of the topic by holding the main branches together in one place
- the design can feel more ownable because it has fewer generic listing patterns, stronger illustration use, and more purposeful section anatomy

# Healthy Living Navigation A/B Vertical Pass 2026-04-30

## Scope
- Focus the Healthy Living guide navigation exploration on two stronger options:
  - `A. Persistent Chapter Rail`
  - `B. Vertical Chapter Browser`
- Make the guide chapters read as the primary vertical planning structure.
- Show both the topic guide page and a supporting subpage for each approach.

## Direction
- Keep the topic guide as the canonical home.
- Make the chapter sequence the main visual and structural object.
- Keep supporting articles attached to chapters, not exposed as a full navigation tree.
- Use real Healthy Eating content labels where possible:
  - `Start Here`
  - `Eating Basics`
  - `Build Your Plate`
  - `Choose Foods`
  - `Plan & Cook`
  - `Key Nutrients`
  - `Real Life`
  - `Health Goals`
  - `Tools & Recipes`

## Option A: Persistent Chapter Rail
- Use a chapter-only left rail on the guide page.
- Keep the rail present on supporting pages so each article has visible guide context.
- Supporting pages show:
  - `Part of Chapter X`
  - `More in this chapter`
  - `Next guide step`
- This option gives the strongest orientation while reading.
- Risk: it may feel heavy on smaller topics unless the rail collapses well on mobile.

## Option B: Vertical Chapter Browser
- Use the topic guide as a browseable chapter index.
- Each chapter preview shows:
  - core guide lesson
  - one or two supporting articles
  - relevant tools or resource links
- Supporting pages use compact chapter context rather than a full persistent rail.
- This option is more browseable and makes article/resource surfacing clearer on the guide page.
- Risk: supporting pages may feel less anchored than Option A unless the parent chapter context is strong.

## Recommendation to Test
- Treat `A` as the stronger reading/navigation model.
- Treat `B` as the stronger overview/browsing model.
- The likely final direction may combine them:
  - a vertical chapter browser at the top of the topic guide
  - a compact persistent chapter rail once users are inside guide reading or support pages

## Direction
- keep `Conditions` fixed, chapter-led, and authority-first
- use `Healthy Living` as the adaptive hub candidate
- keep the six-guide framework stable:
  - `Eat better`
  - `Move more`
  - `Sleep better`
  - `Manage stress`
  - `Quit tobacco or vaping`
  - `Manage weight`
- let the Healthy Living hub feel broad and exploratory on arrival
- remove the bottom selector
- let the six Healthy Living themes become the visible chooser
- let a user click one theme and rebalance the hub in place

## Revised route direction
- use the earlier middle direction as the base
- keep the page broad and exploratory on arrival
- surface the six themes as visible featured tiles/cards
- treat those six tiles as both framework and chooser
- when one is selected, make that theme become the lead guide area at the top
- let the supporting content underneath rebalance around it

## Selected comparison state
- selected interest: `Eat better`
- selected state should promote:
  - `Healthy Eating`
  - two or three chapter starting points
  - recipes
  - label-reading
  - meal-planning
  - one lifestyle or editorial feature
  - quieter non-selected themes still visible on the page

## Key decision
- the adaptive pattern is better suited to `Healthy Living` than to `Conditions`
- the bottom chooser is not needed on this page
- the six-guide framework should become the interaction model itself
- the selected theme should become the lead guide area rather than only updating content lower on the page

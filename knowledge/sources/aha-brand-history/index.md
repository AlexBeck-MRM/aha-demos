## What these sources currently establish
- The AHA did not start as a mass public charity brand. It started as a professional scientific society in 1924 and became a broad public-health movement in 1948.
- The brand’s deepest job has been trust transfer: turning specialist science into public action, fundraising, advocacy, education, and behavior change.
- The website archive now has a cleaner visual progression from `1997` to `2018`, with viable checkpoints in `1997`, `2000`, `2001`, `2005`, `2008`, `2010`, `2014` and `2018`.
- Red is central, but the modern masterbrand does not treat it as an all-over wash. The current system treats red as a strong accent anchored by white space, black copy, and a tightly controlled heart-and-torch mark.
- `Go Red for Women` made red social and participatory, not just institutional, and helped move the AHA into cultural visibility beyond classic nonprofit communications.
- Public evidence points to Dalton on the 2014 emotional repositioning era, CP+B Boulder and Edelman on the 2017 AOR shift, and a tightly governed in-house brand team on the 2018 masterbrand system.

## Retrieval note
When making claims about AHA brand history, prefer at least:
- one official AHA or NHLBI source for the historical fact
- one archival or external publication for the interpretation of the later brand shift

If authorship of the exact modern mark or masterbrand system matters, treat it as only partially resolved. Current public sources do not cleanly name one outside design firm as the sole creator of the 2018 system.

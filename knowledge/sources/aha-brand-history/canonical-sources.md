# AHA Brand History Canonical Sources

## Official AHA and AHA-owned sources

1. `History of the American Heart Association`
   URL: `https://www.heart.org/en/about-us/history-of-the-american-heart-association`
   Why it matters:
   - establishes the 1924 founding, the 1948 reorganization, and the official milestone timeline
   - provides the historic logo image and 1924 incorporation seal used in this folder

2. `A Century of Heart`
   URL: `https://www.heart.org/en/around-the-aha/a-century-of-heart`
   Why it matters:
   - frames the centennial story the way AHA now tells it
   - surfaces the centennial article links and visual language around AHA’s first century

3. `The AHA 2030 Impact Goal`
   URL: `https://professional.heart.org/en/science-news/the-aha-2030-impact-goal`
   Why it matters:
   - confirms the current mission line, `a relentless force for a world of longer, healthier lives`
   - shows how the brand has widened from disease prevention to healthy life expectancy and equity

4. `Go Red for Women: About Us`
   URL: `https://www.goredforwomen.org/en/about-go-red-for-women`
   Why it matters:
   - confirms that AHA created Go Red for Women in 2004
   - explains how AHA adopted the red dress symbol in alignment with NHLBI

5. `Life Is Why`
   URL: `https://www.heart.org/en/get-involved/ways-to-give/life-is-why`
   Why it matters:
   - shows the emotional giving platform still lives inside the AHA ecosystem
   - preserves the phrasing around living healthier, longer lives through personal motivation

6. `2024 AHA Branding & Communications Guide`
   URL: `https://www.heart.org/-/media/Files/Professional/Quality-Improvement/Quality-Award-Toolkit/2024/2024AHABrandGuidelines.pdf`
   Why it matters:
   - shows current partner-facing brand control
   - useful proof that logo usage remains tightly governed

7. `2018 AHA Masterbrand Guidelines (hosted copy)`
   URL: `https://digitalmedia.vnr1.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/Brand_Guidlines-Release-12-19-2018.pdf`
   Why it matters:
   - strongest accessible document on the newer masterbrand logic
   - establishes the `masterbrand` framing, `Lub Dub` type, `AHA Red`, accent logic, photography rules, and program tethering
   Provenance note:
   - a DuckDuckGo result surfaced an official AHA path, `https://www.heart.org/-/media/Files/Brand/AHA_Masterbrand_Guidelines_Condensed.pdf`, but that URL returned `403` on `2026-04-15`
   - this hosted copy appears to preserve the same 2018 guidelines and is used here with that caveat

## External publications and agency references

8. `Dalton Agency: American Heart Association`
   URL: `https://daltonagency.com/work/american-heart-association/`
   Why it matters:
   - public agency claim that Dalton helped breathe new life into the AHA brand
   - useful source for the 2014-era shift away from science-only messaging toward emotional connection

9. `Adweek: The American Heart Association Just Named Its First Global Advertising Agency of Record`
   URL: `https://www.adweek.com/agencies/the-american-heart-association-just-named-its-first-global-advertising-agency-of-record/`
   Why it matters:
   - confirms that in 2017 AHA named CP+B Boulder as global advertising AOR and Edelman as global PR AOR
   - records AHA’s stated goal to reimagine the brand in more relevant and contemporary terms

10. `NHLBI: About The Heart Truth and The Red Dress`
    URL: `https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/education/heart-truth/about`
    Why it matters:
    - confirms NHLBI created The Heart Truth in 2002 and introduced the Red Dress as the national symbol
    - clarifies the federal ownership of the Red Dress symbol and AHA’s later collaboration around it

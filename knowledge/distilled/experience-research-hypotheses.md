# Experience Research Hypotheses

## What this file is for
This is the fast retrieval layer for the merged experience-research PDF. Use it when you need evidence to create or validate ideas around homepage clarity, content structure, navigation, search, editorial packaging, donation timing, or volunteering paths.

## What this source adds to the project
- It gives concrete behavioral logic behind several themes already present in discovery:
  - trust grows through clarity, not just authority
  - search and navigation need systemic improvement, not cosmetic cleanup
  - engagement works better when it is timed and contextual
  - editorial expression only works when information scent remains intact
- It turns broad ambitions into usable validation checks for specific surfaces.

## High-confidence validation rules
- Symptom and condition pages should answer the core question, surface urgency early, and keep next steps obvious.
- High-intent health content should use explanatory visuals and calm hierarchy rather than decorative lifestyle imagery.
- Navigation should work as a system of menu, local routes, breadcrumbs, labels, search, and recovery paths.
- Search should support lay language, adjacent concepts, and off-ramps when a query is imperfect.
- The homepage should explain what AHA does, who it helps, and where to go next before fragmenting attention across too many messages.

## Medium-confidence validation rules
- Donation should generally follow value, trust, and context rather than interrupting high-information journeys too early.
- Staged engagement is stronger than asking every user to make the same high-commitment move immediately.
- Editorial content may carry more curation, rhythm, and visual energy, but it still needs clear topical framing and repeatable logic.

## Best use cases
- Validating homepage hero and first-load decisions
- Arguing for simpler, more task-led navigation language
- Shaping scoped search, result recovery, and breadcrumb behavior
- Framing donation and volunteering as a progression instead of a blunt ask
- Giving Route A editorial ideas a more grounded UX rationale

## Confidence note
Treat the symptom/condition, findability, and homepage sections as the strongest.
Treat the donation/volunteering page as solid but slightly softer.
Treat the editorial page as directional until a higher-resolution export appears.

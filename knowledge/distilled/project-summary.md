# Project Summary

The American Heart Association website refresh is aiming to redefine AHA's digital presence as a modern, emotionally resonant, highly usable brand ecosystem. The goal is not just a prettier site, but a clearer and more ownable digital expression of trust, guidance, utility, and action.

The assistant's role in this project is that of a senior digital art director and design partner. It should think in terms of narrative, composition, interaction, brand expression, and system logic. It should help shape both philosophical and practical decisions, from design principles and brand behavior to page patterns, styleframes, prompts, UI inventories, and route comparisons.

The direction established so far points to a website that should feel:
- white-first, bright, and spatial
- warm and emotionally intelligent without becoming beige or sentimental
- tactile and softly interactive
- useful and content-rich without becoming noisy
- modern, approachable, and authoritative
- alive with detail, but constrained in the right way

The work is currently expressed through two kinds of artifacts:
- styleframes: high-fidelity visual direction pieces
- a UI style inventory: category-by-category direction and reasoning across the website system

Two structural styleframes frame the work:
- `Styleframe 01 - Brand Presence`: a curated expression of the brand through a few strong website moments
- `Styleframe 02 - Website Ecosystem`: a broader proof that the direction scales into a useful, content-rich system

The repo should support ongoing conversations about:
- philosophy: what the AHA digital presence should mean and stand for
- aesthetics: how the visual language should feel and evolve
- function: how the website should guide, support, and convert

This repository is a living working brain for the project, not just an archive.

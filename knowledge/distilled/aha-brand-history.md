# AHA Brand History

## Core arc
- The AHA began in `1924` as a professional scientific society and became a true public-facing movement brand in `1948`, when it reorganized into a voluntary health organization.[1]
- Its brand role has always been broader than awareness. The AHA brand translates science into public trust, behavior change, fundraising, advocacy and community action.
- The modern system is the result of layered shifts, not one single rebrand event:
  - `2004`: `Go Red for Women` gives AHA a social, cultural awareness platform around women’s heart health.[2][3]
  - `2014`: the `life is why` era pushes the brand toward emotional motivation.[4][5]
  - `2017`: CP+B Boulder and Edelman are named first global AORs to reimagine the brand in more contemporary terms.[6]
  - `2018`: the masterbrand guidelines formalize tighter identity discipline, custom type, accent-red behavior and stronger masterbrand control.[7]
- The current mission language, `a relentless force for a world of longer, healthier lives`, broadens the brand from disease-focused prevention into healthier life expectancy and equity.[8]

## What matters for the refresh
- Keep AHA authoritative. This is not a generic wellness brand.
- Keep red strategic. The strongest evidence supports a white-led system with red as emphasis, urgency and rally point, not constant fill.
- Preserve umbrella-brand clarity. The AHA has spent years pulling programs back toward the masterbrand.
- Let emotion serve action. The brand is strongest when human stakes and practical next steps appear together.

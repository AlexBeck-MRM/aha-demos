# AHA Medical Soft Spatial Colour Block Stress Test Prompt

## Use
Use this prompt to stress test the accepted `Medical Soft Spatial - Colour Block` icon style with 12 new AHA-relevant subjects.

This is not a replacement style. It should keep the accepted v4 language from `design/ui-style-inventory/icons.md` and test whether the system can handle more complex, less obvious subjects without drifting into clutter, random support shapes, realism, or cute 3D.

## Source Style
- Style guide: `design/ui-style-inventory/icons.md`
- Accepted reference sheet: `design/generated/icons/medical-soft-spatial-colour-block/aha-medical-soft-spatial-colour-block-12-sheet-v4.png`

## Stress Test Goal
Test whether the icon language holds across:

- medical devices
- cards and records
- abstract health states
- prevention and recovery subjects
- action-led subjects
- topic icons that need a little semantic detail

The sheet should reveal whether the system can stay consistent when the subject is not as immediately iconic as blood pressure, heart rhythm, sleep, or healthy eating.

## Prompt
Create a single raster image reference sheet for an AHA health nonprofit icon system.

Canvas:
Square image. Pure white `#ffffff` background. Arrange exactly twelve icons in a 4-column by 3-row grid, equal scale, generous gutters, each icon inside an invisible square container. Do not draw containers, borders, labels, headings, numbers, logos, UI chrome, watermark, or visible text.

Style:
Use one style only: `Medical Soft Spatial - Colour Block`. Keep the exact accepted AHA style: calm, mature, medical, white-surface friendly, softly dimensional, and built from broad matte masses. Use shallow three-quarter top view, shallow side planes, gentle overlaps, and pale close shadows. The icons should be small designed UI objects, not scenes, not product renders, not toy-like 3D, and not flat line icons.

Shape rule:
Every visible shape must be the subject, a physically attached part of the subject, the single information signal, or a pale contact shadow. Do not use decorative backplates, random bases, blobs, puddles, halos, wedges, slabs, filler surfaces, or arbitrary support shapes.

Colour:
Keep 65-75% of each icon neutral: off-white, cool bone, pale grey, soft silver, taupe grey. Add one broad muted subject colour only where it improves recognition: blue-grey, sage, pale cream, tobacco tan only if medically relevant, restrained AHA red for information signals. Red must be a separated signal, not decoration. No saturated colour, black, rainbow palette, gradients, or material texture.

Detail:
Default to 2-3 main masses per icon. Allow 4-5 large semantic masses only when the subject becomes unclear without them. Detail is allowed when it explains meaning, but it must be large, structural, and readable at 24-32 px. No labels, numbers, letters, currency symbols, small dials, laces, hand lines, slots, food grains, fabric weave, glass shine, reflections, skin, hair, individual fingers, faces, ellipsis dots, decorative dots, surface speckles, seeds, perforations, gauge ticks, bubbles, smoke wisps, or measurement marks.

Lighting:
Very soft diffuse upper-front light. Shadows are pale, short, blurred, and close. No hard cast shadows, dark contact shadows, heavy ambient occlusion, black interiors, glossy highlights, or product-render drama.

Subject construction:
- Medication routine: simple pale blister pack or pill organizer with one neutral capsule and one small red reminder dot/check. No text, numbers, or tiny pill grid.
- Cholesterol / lab panel: neutral lab card or sample tube with one broad pale yellow-cream sample cue and one small red data point. No labels, numbers, graph axes, or measurement ticks.
- Blood sugar / diabetes check: compact neutral glucose meter with one red blood drop and one muted blue-grey strip. No screen numbers, labels, or tiny device marks.
- Stroke awareness: neutral head or brain-health tile with one clear red asymmetry/alert cue and a small blue-grey support plane only if attached. No face detail, no brain texture, no warning triangle.
- Heart failure self-check: neutral bathroom scale or simple monitoring pad with one small red heart signal. No numbers, readouts, or extra device controls.
- Appointment prep: blue-grey folder or calendar card with stethoscope loop or small red tab. No date numbers, labels, ellipsis dots, or message bubbles.
- Care team: two or three neutral connected care objects, such as stethoscope, folder, and small heart card, grouped as one cluster. Avoid people, faces, hands, or generic teamwork blobs.
- Vaccination / prevention: neutral syringe or vaccine vial with a simple shield or red heart signal. No needle detail beyond one clean large form, no labels, no glass shine.
- Cardiac rehab / recovery: neutral step platform or rehab band with one red heart/pulse cue. No running figure, no shoe, no gym scene.
- Advocacy / policy: neutral megaphone or document card with one red heart/signal. No text, podiums, flags, crowds, or political symbols.
- Learning / education: neutral open book or learning card with one small red heart bookmark/tab. No text lines, letters, pencil clutter, or classroom scene.
- Symptom tracking: neutral checklist card or simple tracker tile with one red marker and one blue-grey check shape. No written list, no rows of tiny marks, no numbers.

Avoid:
Realism, photorealism, realistic miniatures, product rendering, object-true materials, detailed devices, detailed cards, skin tones, faces, hair, individual fingers, labels, text, numbers, letters, currency symbols, random support shapes, decorative backplates, blobs, halos, generic health-tech icon tropes, oversized warning symbols, rainbow palette, saturated colours, heavy bevels, glossy highlights, cute toy proportions, dense scenes, and stock icon-library sameness.

Quality check:
- Exactly twelve new icons appear in a 4 x 3 grid.
- None of the accepted v4 subjects are repeated directly.
- The sheet still reads as the accepted AHA `Medical Soft Spatial - Colour Block` style.
- Every icon has a clear subject read at small size.
- Any added detail clarifies meaning and does not become texture or decoration.
- Red is used as a separate information signal.
- No random shapes appear.

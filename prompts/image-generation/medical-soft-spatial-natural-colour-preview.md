# AHA Medical Soft Spatial Natural Colour Preview Prompt

## Use
Use this prompt to generate a natural-colour variant of the AHA Medical Soft Spatial icon sheet. This is a style exploration branch from the current grey-led v9 direction, not a replacement.

The goal is to test whether more object-true colour improves recognition without making the set playful, noisy, or lifestyle-led.

## Direction
Use one icon language only: `Medical Soft Spatial - Natural Colour`.

The icons should keep the same AHA spatial foundation: clean medical objects, soft diffuse lighting, broad matte forms, gentle three-quarter view, restrained detail, and white UI compatibility. The change is colour behaviour: use more natural object colour where it helps meaning, while keeping AHA red as a signal and keeping the overall palette calm.

## Colour Strategy
- Use natural colour as broad, muted object colour, not decoration.
- Each icon may use one or two natural colour areas if they help recognition.
- Keep at least half of each icon grounded in AHA-compatible neutrals: off-white, cool bone, light grey, pale warm grey, neutral medium grey, and taupe grey.
- Use AHA red for signal, urgency, pulse, AED, check, alert, bookmark, or selected state. Do not turn every red object into a brand-red object.
- Allow object-true colours when they are useful:
  - muted medical blue grey for fabric, sleep, calm, appointment, and care objects
  - muted sage or leaf green for produce, plants, path, community support, and healthy movement
  - restrained apple red or tomato red for food only when it reads naturally
  - tobacco tan for smoking/vaping
  - controlled red liquid for science or medicine
  - pale cream or oatmeal for grains/food mass
  - silver/neutral grey for devices, metal, microscope, blister packs, stethoscope, coins, and gauges
- Avoid rainbow distribution. The sheet should not feel like 16 unrelated colourful stickers.
- Avoid candy colours, saturated blue, saturated green, orange-heavy warmth, beige/brown dominance, playful gradients, and bright lifestyle palettes.

## Detail And Realism
- Realism means believable object colour and proportion, not photographic detail.
- Keep a strict detail budget: one main object, one signal, and at most one support cue.
- Use broad colour masses. Avoid detailed produce texture, fabric weave, skin tone, hair, food grains, tiny labels, tiny markings, and product details.
- Design every icon to work at 24-32 px: clear silhouette, simple value contrast, and no reliance on tiny detail.

## Lighting
- Use broad diffuse upper-front lighting, like a soft studio lightbox.
- Shadows should be pale, soft, short, and close to the object.
- Avoid harsh cast shadows, hard contact shadows, heavy ambient occlusion, dark interiors, and dramatic product-render contrast.
- Lift dark greys so they feel charcoal-taupe or medium grey, not black.

## Human Forms
- Keep people rare, abstract, and secondary.
- CPR can use a simplified neutral responder and mannequin/torso.
- Volunteer/community can use linked hands or neutral relationship forms.
- Appointment/care must be object-led: stethoscope plus appointment card, blank calendar chip, care folder, clipboard, or chat bubble. No clinician/patient scene.
- Avoid faces, skin tones, hair, individual fingers, expressive bodies, or character scenes.

## Subject Colour Notes
Use 16 icons in this order:

1. Blood pressure: light grey cuff, charcoal-taupe rubber bulb/tube, pale gauge face, small red needle or reading signal.
2. Heart rhythm: neutral off-white heart or surface, red pulse line only.
3. CPR / AED: neutral mannequin/responder, red AED case or AED signal, minimal clinical grey support.
4. Acute warning signs: off-white warning triangle, red exclamation, pale grey side plane.
5. Healthy eating: off-white bowl, broad muted greens, natural apple/tomato red, pale cream food mass; no detailed grains or texture.
6. Movement: light grey shoe, muted sage path or small plant cue; avoid sporty saturated colour.
7. Sleep: neutral bed/pillow, muted clinical blue grey blanket/window, soft grey frame.
8. Mental wellbeing: neutral head/form, muted blue-grey breath/calm cue, optional pale support tint; no facial detail.
9. Quit smoking / vaping: white cigarette/vape, tobacco tan filter, grey ash/device, red slash or quit cue.
10. Volunteer / community: neutral linked hands/forms, muted sage or soft blue support cue; avoid red floating check or heart.
11. Research / science: light grey microscope, pale glass, controlled red liquid or sample cue.
12. Donate / support: off-white card/receipt/folder, grey coin, small red tab/dot/seal; no currency symbols and no heart-in-hand.
13. Medication routine: white bottle, silver blister pack, red/white capsule; no labels or numbers.
14. Appointment / care conversation: stethoscope plus blank appointment card, blue-grey folder, blank calendar chip, or chat bubble; no people.
15. Habit tracker / progress: pale calendar/card, red check, muted sage progress path; no numerals.
16. Health education / learning module: off-white pages, muted blue-grey cover, red bookmark; no text.

## Prompt
Create a single raster image reference sheet for an AHA health nonprofit medical-spatial icon system using a controlled natural-colour variant.

Canvas:
Square image. Pure white #ffffff background. Arrange exactly 16 icons in a strict 4-column by 4-row grid. Every icon must sit inside its own invisible square container. All square containers must be equal size with even spacing and generous gutters. Do not draw containers, borders, labels, headings, numbers, logos, UI chrome, watermark, or visible text.

Style:
Use one style only: Medical Soft Spatial - Natural Colour. Clean, calm, gently dimensional medical objects. Use believable object proportions, broad matte surfaces, soft spatial side planes, and a consistent gentle three-quarter view. Keep the execution simplified and icon-like rather than photorealistic. Use more natural object colour than the grey-led AHA sheet, but keep the palette restrained, desaturated, and AHA-compatible.

Colour:
Use natural colour where it helps identify the object, but keep the system calm. Use light neutrals as the base, then add one or two broad natural colour areas per icon. Use muted greens, clinical blue greys, tobacco tan, controlled red liquid, pale food cream, silver greys, and restrained natural red. Use AHA red only as a clean signal or brand-relevant cue. Avoid rainbow variety, bright lifestyle colour, candy colour, saturated blue/green, broad orange/brown warmth, and decorative colour accents.

Lighting:
Use soft diffuse upper-front lighting. Shadows must be pale, short, close, and gentle. No hard cast shadows, no heavy contact darkness, no black interiors, no dramatic product-render contrast.

Human forms:
Keep people abstract and minimal. No skin tones, faces, hair, individual fingers, or portraits. Appointment/care must be object-led with stethoscope plus appointment card/folder/chat/calendar cue, no people.

Grid content:
Row 1: blood pressure cuff/gauge; heart rhythm; CPR/AED; acute warning sign.
Row 2: healthy eating; movement; sleep; mental wellbeing.
Row 3: quit smoking/vaping; volunteer/community; research/science; donate/support.
Row 4: medication routine; appointment/care conversation; habit tracker/progress; health education/learning module.

Avoid:
Photorealism, grey-only icons, rainbow icon set, candy colours, playful stickers, toy-like 3D, clay render, harsh shadows, heavy ambient occlusion, black interiors, saturated blue, saturated green, orange/brown dominance, wood-heavy lifestyle props, decorative clutter, detailed food texture, skin tones, faces, hair, individual fingers, portrait scenes, clinician-patient appointment scene, labels, text, numbers, letters, currency symbols, dollar signs, product marks, tiny gauge markings, tiny calendar markings, large red hearts, floating hearts, emoji hearts, dense props, full scenes, and stock icon sameness.

## Quality Check
- Exactly 16 icons appear in a 4 x 4 grid.
- The sheet uses one style only.
- Natural colours improve object recognition without making the set playful or busy.
- The icons still feel AHA: clean, calm, medical, useful, and restrained.
- AHA red remains a signal, not a default fill.
- Appointment/care is object-led with no people.
- Shadows are soft and pale.
- Icons remain readable at small UI sizes.

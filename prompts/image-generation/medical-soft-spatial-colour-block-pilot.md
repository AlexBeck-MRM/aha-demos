# AHA Medical Soft Spatial Colour Block Pilot Prompt

## Use
Use this prompt to test a smaller six-icon pilot for the AHA Medical Soft Spatial icon direction.

This prompt intentionally avoids the language of realism, naturalism, product rendering, and object-true material. The goal is not more realistic icons. The goal is soft dimensional colour blocking: enough colour to identify the subject, but still simple, designed, calm, and system-led.

## Direction
Use one icon language only: `Medical Soft Spatial - Colour Block`.

The icons should feel like simplified AHA medical UI objects made from broad matte colour planes. They should have a gentle spatial feel, but their meaning should come from silhouette, object choice, and one or two flat colour blocks, not from surface detail.

## Exact Style Characteristics
This is the working direction from the stronger v2 pilot.

- The icon is a small designed object, not a scene. It should usually contain one main object, one support shape, and one information signal.
- The camera angle is shallow three-quarter top view: enough to show side planes and depth, but not enough to feel like a product render.
- Forms are mature and simple: rounded medical-object geometry, softened cylinders, clean block planes, and practical proportions. Avoid inflated toy shapes, oversized heads, expressive poses, or mascot-like characters.
- The colour logic is neutral first. Most of the icon is off-white, cool bone, pale grey, soft silver, or taupe grey. Colour is added as one broad local mass that helps recognition.
- Red is a signal, not decoration. It should sit as a clear separate mark, object, liquid, pulse, AED, alert cue, or slash. Do not bury red inside brown, grey, or muddy shadow areas.
- Natural object colour is allowed only when it simplifies recognition: blue-grey for fabric/device blocks, sage for produce/support, pale cream for food/paper, tobacco tan only for smoking cues, controlled red for AHA signals.
- Surfaces stay flat and clean. Do not render material truth: no glass shine, metal reflections, fabric weave, food grains, seeds, pores, dots, bubbles, product labels, or tiny repeated marks.
- Shadows are environmental, not dramatic. Use pale contact shadows close to the object, with soft diffuse upper-front lighting and no hard cast shadows.
- Detail is budgeted for small UI use. At 24-32 px the icon should still read from silhouette, broad colour, and one red cue. Anything that would only work large should be removed.
- People should be avoided where possible. If a person is necessary for action clarity, reduce them to neutral abstract body/hands with no face, no hair, no skin tone, no fingers, and no character expression.

## Core Guardrails
- Use colour as a flat local mass, not as material rendering.
- Use one main colour block per icon, with a second support colour only when it clearly improves recognition.
- Keep 60-70% of each icon neutral: off-white, cool bone, pale grey, light stone, soft medium grey, taupe grey.
- Use AHA red only as a signal: pulse, AED, alert, check, quit slash, small tab, or reading mark.
- Avoid using colour to describe texture, shading, grain, fabric, food detail, skin, hair, labels, tiny marks, reflections, or realism.
- Keep the lighting soft and even. Shadows should be pale, short, and barely there.
- Use simple rounded geometry and shallow side planes. No heavy bevels, no hard cast shadows, no dark interiors.
- Every icon must read at 24-32 px. If a detail would disappear at that size, remove it.
- Do not use decorative dots, ellipsis marks, surface speckles, grains, seeds, perforations, gauge ticks, or tiny repeated marks. These make the style too detailed.

## Colour Behaviour
Use these as broad blocks only:

- Muted blue grey: cuff, sleep blanket, care folder, stethoscope tube.
- Muted sage: produce, movement path, support cue.
- Restrained red: AHA signal, AED, pulse, alert, quit slash, red capsule, bookmark.
- Tobacco tan: cigarette/vape filter only.
- Pale cream: food mass or paper surface.
- Soft silver grey: devices, microscope, blister pack, gauge, coin.

Do not use candy colour, saturated colour, rainbow variety, decorative accents, colour gradients, texture colour, or tiny multicolour object parts.

## Six Subjects
Use exactly six icons:

1. Blood pressure
2. CPR / AED
3. Healthy eating
4. Sleep
5. Appointment / care conversation
6. Research / science

## Prompt
Create a single raster image reference sheet for an AHA health nonprofit icon system using a soft dimensional colour-block style.

Canvas:
Square image. Pure white #ffffff background. Arrange exactly six icons in a 3-column by 2-row grid. Every icon must sit inside its own invisible square container with equal size, consistent scale, even spacing, and generous gutters. Do not draw containers, borders, labels, headings, numbers, logos, UI chrome, watermark, or visible text.

Style:
Use one style only: Medical Soft Spatial - Colour Block. The icons should be calm, mature, medical, and softly dimensional. Build each icon from broad simplified shapes with matte local colour blocks. Use shallow side planes, gentle overlaps, and pale close shadows only. The icons should feel designed and system-led, not realistic, not illustrative scenes, and not cute toy objects. Surfaces must be clean and uninterrupted: no speckles, no grains, no visible texture, no tiny repeated details.

Colour:
Keep 60-70% of each icon neutral. Add one broad subject colour block where it helps recognition. Add one small AHA red signal only when it carries information. Colours should be muted and calm: blue grey, sage green, pale cream, soft silver grey, tobacco tan, restrained red. Avoid rainbow palette, saturated colours, decorative colour, gradients, food texture, fabric texture, skin colour, and material rendering.

Lighting:
Use very soft diffuse upper-front light. Shadows are pale, short, blurred, and close to the object. No dark contact shadows, no hard cast shadows, no black interiors, no heavy ambient occlusion, no product-render drama.

Detail:
Use a strict 24-32 px detail budget. One main subject, one signal, and at most one support shape. No small numerals, labels, tiny dials, product marks, food grains, fabric weave, glass shine, reflections, skin, hair, individual fingers, faces, scene details, ellipsis dots, decorative dots, surface speckles, repeated marks, seeds, perforations, or gauge ticks.

Subject construction:
- Blood pressure: simple muted blue-grey cuff block, pale gauge face or reading dot, soft grey bulb/tube, one small red needle or mark.
- CPR / AED: neutral simplified responder/torso or hands, neutral mannequin pad, red AED block or AED signal. Keep it abstract and non-character-led.
- Healthy eating: off-white bowl, one large sage produce shape, one restrained red apple/tomato block, one single smooth pale cream food mass. No grains, florets, seeds, dots, texture, or multiple tiny foods.
- Sleep: neutral bed/pillow shape, one muted blue-grey blanket/window block, small neutral moon cue if needed.
- Appointment / care: object-led only. Stethoscope plus blank appointment card, blue-grey care folder, blank calendar chip, or plain chat bubble. No people, no ellipsis dots, no message dots, no marks on the card.
- Research / science: soft grey microscope and one simple flask/sample block with controlled red liquid. No glass reflections, bubbles, measurement marks, or tiny lab detail.

Avoid:
Realism, photorealism, realistic miniatures, product rendering, object-true material, detailed food, detailed fabric, skin tones, faces, hair, individual fingers, clinician-patient scenes, labels, text, numbers, letters, currency symbols, tiny markings, ellipsis dots, decorative dots, surface speckles, grains, seeds, perforations, gauge ticks, harsh shadows, black interiors, heavy bevels, glossy highlights, clay render, toy-like 3D, cute proportions, decorative props, dense scenes, rainbow palette, saturated colours, and stock icon-library sameness.

## Quality Check
- Exactly six icons appear in a 3 x 2 grid.
- The style reads as soft dimensional colour blocks, not realism.
- Colour improves recognition without adding detail.
- Shadows are softer and lighter than v8/v9.
- Each icon remains simple enough for small UI use.
- The set still feels AHA: calm, medical, restrained, white-surface friendly, and red-as-signal.

# AHA Icon Style Evolution Sheet Prompt

## Use
Use this prompt to generate a single raster reference board comparing three possible evolutions of the AHA icon system. The sheet is for style review, not production icon export. It should help the team judge how much line, fill, neutral tone, and purposeful colour the next Route A icon language can carry before it becomes visually busy.

## Review intent
- Reduce the amount of visual information in icon-led modules.
- Keep Route A warm, human, and approachable without returning to beige-heavy illustration.
- Use the middle `Restrained Solid` direction as the quality anchor: broad simple forms, neutral-first fills, purposeful red, and very little detail.
- Ground most icon mass in white, off-white, cool bone, light stone grey, pale grey stone, taupe-grey, and soft charcoal.
- Find the middle ground between cold clinical grey and overly warm beige. The greys should feel warm enough for the website, but not sandy, tan, clay, brown, or sepia.
- Use AHA red and subject-linked pastel colour only where it transfers information.
- Compare the same 12 subjects across all three styles so the critique is apples-to-apples.

## Style legend
- `Line`: soft taupe/charcoal line icons, slightly chunky, low-detail, calm, and readable at small UI sizes. Use pale fill only as an optional support accent. Do not use black.
- `Restrained Solid`: no-outline flat icons with neutral foundations, broad simple fills, and one purposeful red or subject-linked colour cue. This keeps the current solid-shape foundation but removes scattershot colour.
- `Tonal Cutout`: a quieter hybrid direction using pale neutral filled silhouettes, white negative-space cuts, sparse taupe detail, and one small red information signal. It should feel more ownable than generic line icons but lighter than full solid illustrations.

## Prompt
Create a single raster image reference board for a health nonprofit icon system.

Canvas:
2048x2048 square image. Pure white board background. Build one clean comparison sheet as a strict 6-column by 6-row grid, for exactly 36 total icons. Every icon must live inside its own invisible square container. The square containers should be equal size, evenly spaced, and large enough that no icon feels squeezed, stretched, cropped, or compressed. Do not draw the square containers, cell borders, labels, captions, headings, numbers, logos, UI screenshots, watermark, or any visible text.

Board structure:
Arrange the grid left-to-right in groups of three square cells. Each group of three shows one subject in this style order: Line, Restrained Solid, Tonal Cutout. Put two subjects per grid row.
Grid row 1: blood pressure triplet, heart rhythm triplet.
Grid row 2: CPR/AED triplet, acute warning signs triplet.
Grid row 3: healthy eating triplet, movement triplet.
Grid row 4: sleep triplet, mental wellbeing triplet.
Grid row 5: quit smoking/vaping triplet, volunteer/community triplet.
Grid row 6: research/science triplet, donate/support triplet.
Do not add a seventh row. Do not add extra subjects. Do not split one subject into two rows. There must be exactly 12 subject triplets and exactly 36 icons.

Overall direction:
The icons should feel like an evolution of the American Heart Association website refresh: modern, calm, useful, warm, trustworthy, and easy to scan on white cards and navigation surfaces. Keep every icon low-detail and readable at small sizes. Make the set feel adult and precise, not playful, childish, decorative, or generic wellness stock. Each icon should have one clear subject and no more than one support cue.

Palette:
Use white #ffffff as the board and cell background. Use off-white #f4f3f3, soft white #f5f4f2, cool bone #eeece9, light stone grey #e1e0dc, pale warm grey #d8d9d6, taupe grey #7d7971, soft charcoal #3d3c3a, AHA red #e6001b, and deep red #bf0000. Avoid pure black. Keep the main neutral read light grey and pale stone, not beige. Warm stone #ddd8d2 is allowed only as a tiny warmth cue where needed; do not let it dominate any icon. Secondary pastel tints may appear only when tied to subject meaning: water or air #edf2f3, nature #e7f0eb, soft red support #f6e8e9, and rare food/oat warmth #f5ebdd.

Column 1, Line style:
Use rounded linework in taupe grey and soft charcoal, never black. Keep strokes slightly chunky and simplified, with clear silhouettes and minimal interior marks. Use no more than one pale support fill per icon, and only when it helps recognition. Red should be rare in this column: use it only for a small signal such as a blood pressure reading, AED mark, heart pulse point, check cue, or donation heart.

Column 2, Restrained Solid style:
Use this as the anchor style. Use no-outline flat icon construction. Build each icon from broad light-grey and pale-stone neutral fills first, then add one purposeful red or subject-linked colour cue. Keep the successful broad, solid, simple quality from the previous middle-column generation, but cool the neutrals slightly so they feel like website greys rather than warm beige. Do not use scattered multicolour detail. Separate forms with shape contrast, negative space, and a few taupe/charcoal functional marks rather than outlines. Keep the icon more substantial than a line icon but much simpler than a spot illustration.

Column 3, Tonal Cutout style:
Create a hybrid icon style built from pale neutral silhouettes, white cutout gaps, and sparse taupe/charcoal detail. Use carved negative space to define the inner form where possible. Add one small red information signal per icon when useful, not as decoration. The result should feel calm, soft, and ownable on white surfaces: less skeletal than line icons, lighter than solid illustrations, and very controlled in colour.

Subject guidance:
Blood pressure: cuff, gauge, or arm cuff with one red reading marker.
Heart rhythm: heart and pulse line, with red used for the pulse or heart signal.
CPR/AED: simplified hands, AED pad, or rescue heart; keep it clear but not alarming.
Acute warning signs: simplified alert triangle, body cue, or urgent care symbol; avoid aggressive warning graphics.
Healthy eating: apple, bowl, balanced plate, or nutrition label cue with one red food or check signal.
Movement: one walking figure, shoe, path, or step cue with one red movement marker. Treat movement as one subject only; do not make a separate shoe row and walking row.
Sleep: crescent, pillow, bed, or alarm toggle with a quiet water/air tint if needed.
Mental wellbeing: calm ring, breathing mark, journal, heart-in-circle, or simple support cue.
Quit smoking/vaping: simplified cigarette or vape with a gentle quit mark, red check, or quiet diagonal cue; avoid a huge prohibition symbol.
Volunteer/community: two or three simplified people, hands, heart placard, or community support cue.
Research/science: microscope, flask, data point, or lab card; avoid dense scientific detail.
Donate/support: heart, hand, gift, wallet, or support badge with one red heart or check cue.

Composition rules:
Keep every icon centered inside an invisible square container with enough whitespace to breathe. Each square container must preserve the icon's natural proportions: no squeezing, stretching, flattening, tall compression, or narrow-column distortion. Keep icon proportions consistent across all cells so style differences, not scale changes, drive the comparison. Use simple geometry, rounded corners, and controlled organic curves. Avoid scene backgrounds, people with facial detail, extra props, decorative plants, dotted textures, cables, charts, busy patterning, or environmental settings. Do not crop icons at the cell edges.

Avoid:
Black strokes, harsh contrast, saturated multicolour palettes, blue healthcare palette, green wellness palette, beige or brown dominance, glossy gradients, 3D rendering, realistic lighting, texture, drop shadows, tiny decorative details, full illustrated scenes, mascots, childish proportions, expressive faces, stock icon-library sameness, dense medical diagrams, labels, text, numbers, UI screenshots, watermarks, logos, and any repeated colour accents that do not carry information.

## Quality check
- Same 12 subjects appear in the same order across all 3 columns.
- All 36 icons sit inside equal square invisible containers arranged as a 6 x 6 grid.
- The image contains exactly 36 icons: no extra rows, no duplicate subject rows, and no added subjects.
- No icon appears squeezed, stretched, cropped, or vertically compressed.
- The board contains no visible text, labels, numbers, logos, or UI chrome.
- Every icon is grounded in light grey and pale stone neutral tones first.
- Neutral tones feel warm but not overly warm: no beige, tan, brown, clay, or sepia dominance.
- AHA red appears as a signal, not as repeated decoration or background wash.
- Subject-linked pastel colour is rare and meaningful.
- No pure black, harsh strokes, clutter, decorative filler, or excessive prop detail.
- Icons remain legible when mentally scaled down to navigation, cards, and tool surfaces.

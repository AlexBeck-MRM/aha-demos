# AHA Medical Soft Spatial Colour Block Full Sheet Prompt

## Use
Use this prompt to generate the full 12-icon sheet for the AHA Medical Soft Spatial icon direction.

This prompt follows the stronger v2 pilot. The goal is not realism and not a fully built 3D icon library sheet. The goal is soft dimensional colour blocking: clear health subjects, restrained natural colour where it helps meaning, and a mature white-surface UI feel.

## Direction
Use one icon language only: `Medical Soft Spatial - Colour Block`.

The icons should feel like simplified AHA medical UI objects made from broad matte colour planes. They should have a gentle spatial feel, but their meaning should come from silhouette, object choice, and one or two flat colour blocks, not from surface detail.

## Exact Style Characteristics
- The icon is a small designed object, not a scene. It should usually contain one dominant object cluster and one information signal.
- Keep each icon compressed. Use 2-3 large readable masses, not many small parts. The pilot worked because each icon had a calm silhouette first and details second.
- Use more detail only when the subject becomes unclear without it. Clarifying detail must be large, structural, and semantic: CPR arms/hands show action; a heart or calm cue inside a head shows wellbeing. Decorative or arbitrary detail is still out.
- The camera angle is shallow three-quarter top view: enough to show side planes and depth, but not enough to feel like a product render.
- Forms are mature and simple: rounded medical-object geometry, softened cylinders, clean block planes, and practical proportions. Avoid inflated toy shapes, oversized heads, expressive poses, mascot-like characters, and novelty-symbol proportions.
- The colour logic is neutral first. Most of the icon is off-white, cool bone, pale grey, soft silver, or taupe grey. Colour is added as one broad local mass that helps recognition.
- Red is a signal, not decoration. It should sit as a clear separate mark, object, liquid, pulse, AED, alert cue, or slash. Do not turn large parts of the icon red unless that object is the signal itself.
- Natural object colour is allowed only when it simplifies recognition: blue-grey for fabric/device blocks, sage for produce/support, pale cream for food/paper, tobacco tan only for smoking cues, controlled red for AHA signals.
- Surfaces stay flat and clean. Do not render material truth: no glass shine, metal reflections, fabric weave, food grains, seeds, pores, dots, bubbles, product labels, or tiny repeated marks.
- Shadows are environmental, not dramatic. Use pale contact shadows close to the object, with soft diffuse upper-front lighting and no hard cast shadows.
- Detail is budgeted for small UI use. At 24-32 px the icon should still read from silhouette, broad colour, and one red cue. Anything that would only work large should be removed.
- People should be avoided where possible. If a person is necessary for action clarity, reduce them to neutral abstract body/hands with no face, no hair, no skin tone, no fingers, and no character expression.

## What Worked In The Pilot
- Large uninterrupted forms: cuff, bowl, bed, folder, microscope, flask.
- Colour appeared as one broad recognition block: blue cuff, blue blanket, sage produce, red AED, red apple, red liquid.
- Red stayed visually separate from muddy neutrals and read as a signal.
- The objects had soft depth, but not enough structure to become product renders.
- There were very few internal details: no laces, no labels, no extra symbolic marks, no complex hands, no decorative cuts.
- Each icon had room to breathe in its square.

## Full Sheet Regression To Avoid
- Do not add extra symbolic props just because there are twelve subjects.
- Do not add random support shapes. Every shape must be the subject, a physically attached part of the subject, or the one information signal.
- Do not use decorative backplates, blobs, puddles, halos, wedges, slabs, or filler surfaces.
- Do not make the movement icon a detailed shoe with laces.
- Do not place a blue blob, path, or puddle under the movement icon.
- Do not make volunteer/support into detailed cupped hands or a donation box with slots.
- Do not add leaves, facial profiles, or decorative wellbeing symbols to mental wellbeing.
- Do not place a large green surface, halo, or backing shape behind the wellbeing head.
- Do not make mental wellbeing a blank head with an ear-like green patch. The cue must read as mental/emotional support, not anatomy.
- Do not make quit smoking dominated by a large red prohibition sign.
- Do not make acute warning signs into a heavy triangle-and-card construction.
- Do not make acute warning signs into a strange wedge, rounded triangle, or stacked backplate. Use a plain alert tile.
- Do not make sleep into a bed plus detailed window scene. The bed is the icon; the window is not.
- Do not let red become a large decorative surface across the sheet.
- Do not make every subject more literal than the pilot. Clarity can come from one strong silhouette.

## Clarity Overrides
Some subjects need more than the minimum detail to read correctly.

- CPR / AED should show CPR action, not only a mannequin and AED. Use a simplified kneeling responder with neutral torso, arms, and joined hands pressing on a neutral mannequin chest, plus a red AED case. No face, no hair, no skin tone, no fingers.
- Mental wellbeing should show an integrated meaning cue. Use a neutral head profile with one simple inset signal inside the head: a small sage thought shape with a restrained red heart or calm pulse. Do not use an ear patch, backing disc, halo, leaf cluster, or decorative side blob.
- Detail is acceptable when it explains the subject. It is not acceptable when it becomes texture, decoration, random support structure, or miniature realism.

## Colour Behaviour
Use these as broad blocks only:

- Off-white / cool bone: main neutral object surfaces, paper, beds, bowls, pads.
- Pale grey / soft silver: devices, microscope, gauge, AED support shapes, donation/support objects.
- Taupe grey: tubing, stands, bed frames, neutral bases, shadow-side planes.
- Muted blue grey: cuff, sleep blanket, care folder, stethoscope tube, movement support.
- Muted sage: produce, wellbeing/support cue, community cue.
- Pale cream: smooth food mass, appointment card, research paper, support card.
- Tobacco tan: cigarette or vape cue only.
- Restrained AHA red: pulse, AED, alert, reading mark, quit slash, liquid sample, donation/support signal.

Do not use candy colour, saturated colour, rainbow variety, decorative accents, colour gradients, texture colour, or tiny multicolour object parts.

## Prompt
Create a single raster image reference sheet for an AHA health nonprofit icon system using a soft dimensional colour-block style.

Canvas:
Square image. Pure white #ffffff background. Arrange exactly twelve icons in a 4-column by 3-row grid. Every icon must sit inside its own invisible square container with equal size, consistent scale, even spacing, and generous gutters. Do not draw containers, borders, labels, headings, numbers, logos, UI chrome, watermark, or visible text.

Style:
Use one style only: Medical Soft Spatial - Colour Block. Match the simplicity of the successful six-icon pilot. The icons should be calm, mature, medical, and softly dimensional. Build each icon from 2-3 broad simplified masses with matte local colour blocks. Use a shallow three-quarter top view, shallow side planes, gentle overlaps, and pale close shadows only. The icons should feel designed and system-led, not realistic, not illustrative scenes, not cute toy objects, and not product renders. Surfaces must be clean and uninterrupted: no speckles, no grains, no visible texture, no tiny repeated details. Do not add extra construction, symbolic props, or internal parts to make the subject more literal. No random bases, decorative backplates, halos, blobs, puddles, or filler shapes.

Colour:
Keep 60-70% of each icon neutral. Add one broad subject colour block where it helps recognition. Add one small AHA red signal only when it carries information. Colours should be muted and calm: off-white, cool bone, pale grey, soft silver grey, taupe grey, blue grey, sage green, pale cream, tobacco tan only for smoking cues, restrained AHA red. Avoid rainbow palette, saturated colours, decorative colour, gradients, food texture, fabric texture, skin colour, and material rendering.

Lighting:
Use very soft diffuse upper-front light. Shadows are pale, short, blurred, and close to the object. No dark contact shadows, no hard cast shadows, no black interiors, no heavy ambient occlusion, no product-render drama.

Detail:
Use a strict 24-32 px detail budget. One main subject cluster and one signal. Most icons should use 2-3 main masses. CPR/AED and mental wellbeing may use 4-5 large semantic masses if needed for clarity. Avoid internal lines unless they are the single information signal. No small numerals, labels, tiny dials, laces, hand lines, slots, product marks, food grains, fabric weave, glass shine, reflections, skin, hair, individual fingers, faces, scene details, ellipsis dots, decorative dots, surface speckles, repeated marks, seeds, perforations, gauge ticks, bubbles, or measurement marks.

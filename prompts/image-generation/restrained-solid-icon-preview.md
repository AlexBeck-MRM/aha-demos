# AHA Restrained Solid Icon Preview Prompt

## Use
Use this prompt to generate a single raster reference sheet for the focused icon direction selected from the prior exploration: the restrained solid style. This is a style preview, not production icon export.

## Design rules
- Use one icon language only: no line-style column, no tonal-cutout column, no style comparison.
- Build every icon from a neutral foundation first. The default mass should be light website greys: white, off-white, cool bone, light stone grey, pale warm grey, and muted taupe-grey.
- Keep the greys warm enough to avoid a cold clinical read, but not so warm that the set turns beige, tan, brown, clay, sand, oat, or sepia.
- Make the icons less cute. They should feel mature, useful, and system-ready rather than emoji-like, plush, playful, childish, or mascot-adjacent.
- Keep rounded geometry, but reduce puffy shapes, oversized hearts, soft toy proportions, sleepy cartoon symbols, and decorative charm.
- Do not use a large red heart as the default emotional shortcut. Heart shapes should be small functional signals except when the subject itself is donation/support.
- Across the whole sheet, avoid repeating the same red-heart motif. Use pulse marks, check marks, selected dots, bookmarks, device signals, or compact alert marks where those are more useful.
- Bring colour back selectively. Red is important, but not every accent has to be red.
- Use red for action, urgency, heart-specific meaning, selected states, and emotional anchor points. Red can be a pulse line, gauge needle, alert mark, AED mark, check, bookmark, selected dot, or small heart; it does not need to be a filled heart.
- Use non-red colour only when it clarifies subject matter: nature green for produce or walking context, water/air blue for sleep or calm, soft red tint for support/alert context, and muted warm food tones only when needed.
- Keep colour clean and separated. Do not place red directly inside muddy brown or warm taupe areas unless there is a pale buffer, white cut, clear negative-space separation, or strong value contrast.
- Avoid muddy mixing. Red should sit on white, pale grey, off-white, or a clean separated shape most of the time.
- Use one broad subject-linked colour cue per icon, not scattered micro-accents.
- Keep forms broad, calm, and simple. Each icon should read at card, nav, and tool sizes.
- Use no-outline flat construction. Separate forms through value contrast, negative space, overlap, and a few functional taupe/charcoal marks.
- Keep shadows minimal and broad. No gradients, gloss, render, drop shadows, texture, or decorative detail.
- Sleep needs more contrast than the last preview. Do not make the whole sleep icon blue. Use a neutral pale-grey base, a darker grey or taupe moon/pillow/bed shape, and only a restrained water/air tint as a secondary support cue.

## Prompt
Create a single raster image reference sheet for a health nonprofit restrained-solid icon system.

Canvas:
Square image. Pure white #ffffff background. Arrange exactly 16 icons in a strict 4-column by 4-row grid. Every icon must sit inside its own invisible square container. All square containers must be equal size with even spacing and generous gutters. Do not draw containers, borders, labels, headings, numbers, logos, UI chrome, watermark, or any visible text.

Style:
Use one style only: restrained solid, no-outline flat icons. The icons should feel modern, calm, useful, mature, trustworthy, and easy to scan on white website cards and navigation surfaces. Build each icon from broad neutral fills first, then add one purposeful colour cue where it improves meaning. The visual character should come from simple silhouettes, rounded geometry, soft corners, clean negative space, and a restrained colour system. Avoid cute or emoji-like expression; the set should feel like a serious website system with warmth, not a playful sticker set.

Palette:
Use white #ffffff, off-white #f4f3f3, soft white #f5f4f2, cool bone #eeece9, light stone grey #e1e0dc, pale warm grey #d8d9d6, muted taupe grey #7d7971, and soft charcoal #3d3c3a. Use red #e6001b and deep red #bf0000 only as action, urgency, heart, selection, or emotional signals. Red can be a line, dot, needle, small mark, or compact shape; avoid filling many large heart forms. Use secondary colour sparingly and only when tied to the subject: nature green #e7f0eb, water/air blue #edf2f3, soft red support #f6e8e9, and rare food warmth #f5ebdd. Avoid black. Avoid beige, tan, sand, clay, brown, sepia, muddy taupe, saturated green, saturated blue, or broad warm-brown fields.

Colour behavior:
Red should usually be separated from warm taupe or darker neutral areas by white, pale grey, off-white, a cutout, or enough negative space. Do not bury red inside brown or muddy neutral fills. Keep colour crisp, purposeful, and readable. Not every icon should use red; choose colour based on meaning. Healthy eating can use a clean produce cue, movement can use a nature/path cue, sleep can use a water/air cue, mental wellbeing can use soft support colour, and urgent or heart-specific subjects can use red. Keep red heart forms limited and deliberate.

Sleep icon rule:
The sleep icon must use visible contrast between forms. Do not fill the entire sleep icon with one pale blue colour. Use at least three values: a pale neutral base, a medium neutral or taupe main object, and a small cool support tint. A crescent, pillow, bed, alarm toggle, or moon/window shape is acceptable, but it should feel adult and functional, not cute, dreamy, or nursery-like.

Grid content:
Row 1: blood pressure cuff or gauge with red needle or reading dot; neutral heart rhythm icon with red pulse line, not a full red filled heart; CPR/AED rescue cue with red AED mark, not a cute heart; acute warning sign.
Row 2: healthy eating bowl/plate/apple; movement walking or shoe/path; sleep crescent/pillow/bed with clear neutral contrast; mental wellbeing calm/head/breathing cue with no large red heart.
Row 3: quit smoking/vaping cigarette or vape with gentle quit cue; volunteer/community people or hands with small support signal, not a large heart; research/science microscope or flask; donate/support hand with a controlled heart or donation cue.
Row 4: medication routine pill bottle or schedule cue, no big heart label; appointment/care conversation doctor/patient or chat cue; habit tracker/progress check or path cue; health education book/module/learning cue.

Composition rules:
Keep every icon centered in its square invisible container with natural proportions and enough whitespace to breathe. Keep scale consistent across the grid. Each icon should use one main subject, one action/information signal, and zero or one support shape. Keep people extremely simplified, with no facial detail. Use simple geometry, rounded corners, broad silhouettes, and clean cutouts. Keep hearts smaller and more functional unless the subject is explicitly donation/support; for heart rhythm, prefer a red pulse line on a neutral heart over a large filled red heart. Avoid scene backgrounds, decorative plants, dotted textures, cables, prop clusters, dense charts, busy patterning, and environmental settings.

Avoid:
Line-style icons, tonal cutout variants, multiple styles, labels, text, numbers, logos, UI screenshots, black strokes, harsh contrast, muddy warm neutrals, red placed directly in brown without separation, saturated multicolour accents, blue healthcare palette, green wellness palette, beige dominance, glossy gradients, 3D rendering, realistic lighting, texture, drop shadows, tiny decorative details, full illustrated scenes, mascots, childish proportions, expressive faces, emoji-like hearts, sticker-like cuteness, nursery sleep symbols, plush pillow shapes, stock icon-library sameness, dense medical diagrams, and any colour accents that do not carry information.

## Quality check
- Exactly 16 icons appear in a 4 x 4 grid.
- Only the restrained solid style appears.
- Every icon is grounded in light website greys first.
- Neutral tones are warm but not beige, brown, clay, or muddy.
- Red is separated from darker/warm neutrals and does not sit buried in brown.
- Colour is selective, subject-linked, and not always red.
- Icons feel adult and useful, not cute, emoji-like, or childish.
- Large red hearts are not repeated as the default accent.
- Sleep icon uses multiple neutral values plus a restrained support tint; it is not a single pale-blue icon.
- Icons remain simple, broad, readable, and useful at small UI sizes.

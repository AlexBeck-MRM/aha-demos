# styleframe 01 prompts

use one route section only.

## route a

### goal
create one 16:9 presentation styleframe board for the american heart association website refresh.

### structure lock
- preserve the exact layout, tile count, proportions, positions, and whitespace of the attached wireframe
- use the cleaned visual reference image as the structural guide for spacing, hierarchy, and module read
- use the attached illustration style reference image as the visual guide for any illustrated or icon-led moments
- match the reference image composition closely without copying its literal brand, copy, or subject matter
- do not add, remove, resize, or rearrange modules
- gardena-style brand mosaic
- brand-first, not a homepage screenshot
- centered headline zone above the board
- six fixed modules only
- no standalone nav strip
- embedded ui fragments only
- do not render wireframe labels, slot names, helper text, or orientation notes from the reference images
- do not render route names, presentation labels, or explanatory subtitles anywhere in the board

### locked palette
- use this locked core palette: `#ffffff`, `#f4f3f3`, `#f5f4f2`, `#eeece9`, `#e1e0dc`, `#7d7971`, `#3d3c3a`, `#000000`, `#e6001b`, `#bf0000`
- use secondary pastel tints only inside illustration or topic-specific details: water/air `#edf2f3`, nature `#e7f0eb`, soft red support `#f6e8e9`, rare food/oat warmth `#f5ebdd`

### background and contrast
- use `#ffffff`, `#f4f3f3`, `#f5f4f2`, `#eeece9`, or `#e1e0dc` as the main board background so the lighter cards can sit on top clearly
- keep cards lighter, using `#ffffff`, `#f4f3f3`, or very light neutral surfaces
- do not make the cards taupe, brown, or muddy
- do not use a red ambient bloom, red haze, or inflammatory red gradient in the background
- keep the background pale, neutral, and calm
- let the outer bento tiles sit flat on the board field with little or no cast shadow
- bring visible depth into the ui elements inside the tiles instead: inset panels, floating chips, mini cards, charts, inputs, buttons, and overlays
- use overlap, inset panels, layered surfaces, and controlled shadow inside the ui, not on the whole tile containers

### case rule
- use sentence case only
- no all-caps headings, labels, buttons, or navigation text
- no uppercase button text
- no uppercase navigation items

### required modules
- large upper-left brand statement tile
- upper-middle cta or utility tile
- upper-right branded image tile
- lower-left utility or input tile
- lower-middle cropped interaction or detail tile
- lower-right large question-led or supportive callout tile
- one small signature detail

### copy and visual balance
- keep text sparse
- most tiles should use no more than `3-6` words of headline copy
- allow only one short supporting sentence on the whole board
- at least `2` tiles should be driven primarily by ui moments rather than copy blocks
- no more than `2` tiles should rely on plain photography as their main visual strategy
- at least `1` tile should show visible layered ui depth through inset panels, floating chips, mini cards, or breakout elements
- at least `1` tile should show illustration clearly
- at least `1` tile should show icons clearly
- keep illustration to roughly `30%` of the board or less
- keep the majority of the board driven by photography, ui moments, icons, and content surfaces

### route expression
- warmer
- softer
- more editorial
- more companion-led
- more intimate crops
- more reassuring utility behavior
- more illustration breakout
- more human softness in depth and overlap
- warmer atmosphere through palette balance and softer ui depth, not through a red background bloom

### trust and content rules
- bright white-first with restrained warmth
- use strategic aha red only for primary actions and small accents
- keep buttons rounded, tactile, and pressable
- favor believable caregiving, family, community, recovery, cooking, or clinician imagery with natural light
- let photography support the board, not carry the whole board
- use illustration and icons as clear visual ingredients, not background extras
- keep illustration as a branded support layer rather than the dominant image language
- use the direct illustration style from the attached references
- keep illustration in the same family as the icon system
- use no-outline flat-fill construction with slightly chunky shapes and controlled organic curves
- keep illustration palette to white, off-white, cool bone, light stone grey, taupe-grey, charcoal, AHA red, and deep red, with warm stone and pastel tints used only when the subject needs them
- keep scenes simple, warm, adult, and useful
- let colour arrive through one controlled red action or emotional signal rather than many small accents
- include medically credible information cues where relevant
- include visibly current or up-to-date content cues where relevant
- include clear source visibility when needed
- include authored or reviewed content moments where appropriate

### negative constraints
- do not introduce accent colors outside the locked core palette and allowed secondary pastel tints
- do not let the neutrals drift brown, beige, peach, or blue-healthcare
- do not use pink-red, burgundy-purple, orange-red, blue-gray, or muddy beige drift
- no full homepage screenshots
- no extra tiles outside the wireframe
- no generic card-grid energy
- no cold or sterile healthcare minimalism
- no long explanatory copy blocks
- no plain white panels filled mainly with text
- no unrelated illustration language
- no overly detailed, whimsical, or decorative illustration scenes
- no portrait-style illustration crops or likeness-led family portraits
- no heavy shading, glossy gradients, or modelled faces, hair, hands, or fabric
- no stroked outlines around people, props, furniture, backplates, or ground planes
- no flat white-on-white board with weak contrast
- no shadow-heavy floating bento boxes
- no red atmospheric background glow
